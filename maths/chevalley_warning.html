Chevalley–Warning theorem

Chevalley–Warning theorem is a theorem in number theory concerning solutions of polynomials over finite fields. It can show the existence of a non-trivial solution to a system of polynomial equations, place a lower bound to the amount of solutions and, most curiously, assure that the number of solutions is divisible by the characteristic of the finite field. In this article, we briefly prove the three statements. Unless denoted otherwise, I basically copied Serre's A Course in Arithmetic, with added details.

Prerequisites

The symbol \(p\) represents a prime number, \(q\) a power of a prime number and \(k,f\) and \(n\) natural numbers. The characteristic \(\mathrm{Char}(K)\) denotes the smallest \(n\) for which \(n\cdot 1 = 0\) in \(k\). The symbol \(F_p\) represents \(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z}\).

Fields of powers

If \(K\) is a finite field with \(\mathrm{Char}(K) = p\), then \(K^p = \{x^p \mid x\in K\}\) is its subfield induced by the isomorphism \(\sigma: x\mapsto x^p\).

Number of elements in a field

All finite fields with \(q=p^f\) elements are isomorphic to \(F_q\)

Chevalley–Warning theorem

Statement

Let \(K\) be a finite field of size \(q=p^f\). Consider a family of \(k\) polynomials in \(n\) variables, \(f_j\in K[X_1,\ldots, X_n]\), such that \(\sum_{j=1}^k\deg f_j < n\). Then \[ \lvert V_{K^n}(f_1,\ldots,f_k) \rvert \equiv 0 \pmod p \]

Warning's second theorem

Statement

Let \(K\) be a finite field of size \(q=p^f\). Consider a family of \(k\) polynomials in \(n\) variables, \(f_j\in K[X_1,\ldots, X_n]\), such that \(d = \sum_{j=1}^k\deg f_j < n\). Then either \(V_{K^n}(f_1,\ldots,f_k) = \emptyset\) or \[ \lvert V_{K^n}(f_1,\ldots,f_k) \rvert \geq q^{n-d} \]

Chevalley theorem

Statement

Let \(K\) be a finite field of size \(q=p^f\). Consider a family of \(k\) polynomials in \(n\) variables, \(f_j\in K[X_1,\ldots, X_n]\), such that \(\sum_{j=1}^k\deg f_j < n\) and \(f_j(0)=0\) (they have no constant term). Then there is also a non-trivial common solution.
